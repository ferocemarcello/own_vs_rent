Buy vs. Rent Calculator

The Core Logic

This calculator simulates the financial outcome of buying a home versus renting over a set period. It compares the final net worth of an Owner against a Renter by tracking two main assets for each:

  1. Property Equity (Owner only): The value of the home, which appreciates over time.
  2. Investment Portfolio: A separate investment account (like stocks or bonds) that both the Owner and Renter contribute to and which grows at a specified rate.

How the Simulation Works (Year-by-Year)

  • For the Owner:
    • The Home Value grows by the "Home Yearly Appreciation" rate.
    • The Investment Portfolio grows by the "Investment Return" rate.
    • Each year, the Owner adds their specified "Yearly Investment" to the portfolio.
    • Each year, the Owner's costs (Fixed Expenses, Additional Expenses) are tracked.
  • For the Renter:
    • The Investment Portfolio grows by the "Investment Return" rate.
    • Each year, the Renter adds their specified "Yearly Investment" to the portfolio.
    • Each year, the Renter's costs (Rent, Additional Expenses) are tracked.
  • The "Renter's Savings" (Crucial Assumption): The calculator finds the difference between the Owner's total yearly costs and the Renter's total yearly costs. This "saving" is automatically added to the Renter's investment portfolio each year. This assumes the Renter diligently invests any money saved by not owning.

Final Calculation (End of Period)

At the end of the "Analysis Period," the calculator simulates a final liquidation to compare net worth:

  1. The Owner "sells" the house, paying "Selling Costs." The final home equity is added to their net worth.
  2. Both the Owner and Renter "liquidate" their investment portfolios.
  3. The Capital Gains Tax is calculated *only* on the *growth* (gains) of their portfolios (not the total value). The remaining amount is their net portfolio value.
  4. Final Net Worth = (Net Home Proceeds [Owner only]) + (Net Portfolio Proceeds)

Key Input Fields Explained

General

  • Analysis Period (Years): How many years to run the simulation for.
  • Yearly Inflation (%): Affects "Additional Yearly Expenses" for both Owner and Renter, increasing them each year.

Purchase Inputs (Owner)

  • Home Initial Price: The purchase price of the home.
  • Initial Costs (% and Absolute): One-time fees for buying. Note: In the current version of the calculator, these fields are calculated but not deducted from the owner's portfolio or used in the final net worth calculation.
  • Yearly Expenses with Fixed Term (Absolute): A large, fixed annual cost, typically a mortgage (principal + interest).
  • Years for Fixed Term Expenses: The duration of the fixed expense (e.g., 10-year mortgage term).
  • Yearly Investment during fixed term: Annual amount the Owner invests *while* paying the fixed expense.
  • Additional Yearly Expenses (Absolute / %): Ongoing costs of ownership (property tax, maintenance, insurance). The 'Absolute' part increases with inflation.
  • Home Yearly Appreciation (%): Annual increase in the home's value.
  • Expenses at End of Term (% / Absolute): A one-time fee paid from the Owner's portfolio when the "Fixed Term" ends (e.g., refinancing fee).
  • Selling Costs (% of Final Price): Costs paid when the house is sold at the end of the analysis period.
  • Yearly Investment after fixed term: Annual amount the Owner invests *after* the fixed expense (mortgage) is paid off.

Rental Inputs (Renter)

  • Yearly Rent (Absolute): The starting annual rent.
  • Rent Increase (%/year): How much the rent increases each year.
  • Additional Yearly Expenses (Absolute): Other rental costs (e.g., renter's insurance), which increase with inflation.
  • Yearly Investment: The base amount the Renter invests each year (in addition to any "savings").

Investment Inputs

  • Initial Portfolio Value (Buyer/Renter): The starting amount of money each person has in their investment account at Year 0.
  • Investment Return (%/year): The average annual growth rate of the investment portfolios.
  • Capital Gains Tax (%): The tax rate applied *only* to the gains (profit) in the investment portfolios when they are sold at the end. This is *not* applied to the home sale.
